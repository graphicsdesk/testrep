Last month, University President Lee Bollinger took the stage at the annual John Jay Awards Dinner to address students, alumni, and faculty of Columbia College.

He began his remarks by rattling off a few quick thank-yous, one of which went to outgoing dean of Columbia College, James Valentini.

Then something unexpected happened.

The unprompted standing ovation illustrated the unique position of the College dean in the Columbia ecosystem, as its most public-facing advocate. But the dean’s job could soon change—to the praise of some, and dismay of others—along with the structure writ large of the Faculty of Arts and Sciences and Columbia College.

It's the latest chapter in a saga that has been writing itself for decades, often in flurries of controversy several years apart.
